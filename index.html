Our Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and everything that orbits around it, including planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids.

Mercury

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. Mercury has no atmosphere to retain heat, resulting in extreme temperature variations.

Venus

Often called Earth's sister planet due to similar size and mass. Venus has a thick toxic atmosphere creating a runaway greenhouse effect.

Earth

Our home planet, the only known world with life. Earth's atmosphere and magnetic field protect us from harmful solar radiation.

Mars

Known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface. Mars has polar ice caps and evidence of ancient river valleys and lake beds.

Space Exploration Timeline

Humanity's journey to the stars has been marked by significant milestones:

1957

Sputnik 1 becomes the first artificial satellite to orbit Earth, launched by the Soviet Union.

1961

Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human in space aboard Vostok 1.

1969

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin become the first humans to walk on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission.

1998-2011

Construction and operation of the International Space Station, the largest human-made structure in space.

2021

NASA's Perseverance rover lands on Mars with the Ingenuity helicopter, which performs the first powered flight on another planet.
